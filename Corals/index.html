About Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are vital to marine ecosystems, supporting 25% of ocean species and protecting coastlines from erosion. They also provide food, livelihoods, and medicine for millions of people worldwide.

Featured Species

Stylaster brochi

Stylasters are a type of hydrocoral, which are colonial animals related to jellyfish and sea anemones. They are important members of marine ecosystems, especially in deeper waters where they can form reef habitats.

Pocillopora grandis

Pocillopora, are considered "ecosystem engineers" because they build expansive structures that provide valuable habitat for other species. These structures offer surfaces for marine life to rest and hunt on, and some invertebrates can even burrow into the coral for shelter

Madrepora cytherea

Table corals, with their horizontal growth form, create a unique habitat for marine life. The underside of the table provides a sheltered space for fish and invertebrates to hide from predators or strong currents.
